# Executive Summary — AI OSI Stack v5

**Tagline:** *“The accountability backbone for AI.”*

**Canonical identifiers:** AI OSI Stack v5 (canonical version v5.0-rc, established 2025-11-09). Repository of record: <https://github.com/danielpmadden/ai-osi-stack>. See [CANONICAL_PROVENANCE.yaml](../CANONICAL_PROVENANCE.yaml) and [INTEGRITY_NOTICE.md](../INTEGRITY_NOTICE.md) for deferred-signing status and provenance controls.

## Mission

AI OSI Stack translates civic intent into technical governance so institutions can deploy artificial intelligence with evidence-first accountability. Authored and maintained independently by Daniel P. Madden, the stack unifies ethical, legal, and engineering guardrails into a layered blueprint that any regulator, enterprise, or consortium can audit, extend, or certify. It treats governance as critical infrastructure: open-standard, interoperable, and grounded in verifiable provenance.

## Problem

Organizations face escalating regulatory expectations—EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001, NIST AI Risk Management Framework—without a common substrate to prove what an AI system did and why. Fragmented compliance workflows, undocumented personas, and ad hoc dashboards leave boards, CISOs, and policymakers exposed to regulatory penalties and reputational harm. Existing risk tools produce reports, not signed evidence.

## Solution

AI OSI Stack v5 delivers a nine-layer governance spine (Layer 0 civic mandate through Layer 8 civic participation) plus the AI Epistemic Infrastructure Protocol (AEIP) to encode every decision, model update, and disclosure into machine-verifiable artefacts. The stack bundles:

- Canonical schemas for Decision Rationale Records, Governance Disclosure Statements, and Integrity Ledger Entries ([schemas/](../schemas)).
- A Python “governance spine” runtime (`govspine/`) and validation suites (`npm run validate:aeip`) for operational integration ([README](../README.md), [docs/VERIFICATION_GUIDE.md](../docs/VERIFICATION_GUIDE.md)).
- Audited change logs and integrity reviews documenting canonical authorship and signing posture ([audits/200-repo-wide-integrity-report.md](../audits/200-repo-wide-integrity-report.md)).
- Dashboard scaffolding to surface manifests, custodial roles, and compliance metrics ([docs/governance-dashboard/control-tower-readme.md](../docs/governance-dashboard/control-tower-readme.md)).

## Impact

AI OSI Stack positions executives to:

- Establish civic legitimacy before deployment, aligning with Annex IV disclosures and ISO control planes ([README](../README.md), [docs/LEGAL-AND-POLICY-BRIEF.md](../docs/LEGAL-AND-POLICY-BRIEF.md)).
- Maintain signed provenance chains once deferred signing completes, creating defensible audit trails for litigators and regulators ([INTEGRITY_NOTICE.md](../INTEGRITY_NOTICE.md)).
- Operationalize human dignity safeguards through persona architecture, refusal logic, and data-ethics commitments ([docs/architecture-overview.md](../docs/architecture-overview.md), [DATA-ETHICS.md](../DATA-ETHICS.md)).
- Demonstrate maturity through published audits, verification guides, and advisory security workflows ([docs/VERIFICATION_GUIDE.md](../docs/VERIFICATION_GUIDE.md), [SECURITY.md](../SECURITY.md)).

### Key Takeaways

- **Compliance readiness:** Direct crosswalks to EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, and ISO/IEC 42001 obligations with canonical audit evidence.
- **Provenance assurance:** AEIP schemas and deferred signing workflow bind every artifact to time, author, and ledger.
- **Interoperability:** Open-standard architecture aligns civic mandates, enterprise dashboards, and regulator checkpoints across jurisdictions.
