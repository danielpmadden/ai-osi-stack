# Board Charter Addendum: AI Oversight

## Mandate
- Establishes the Board’s oversight of artificial intelligence strategy, risk management, ethical considerations, and disclosure obligations.
- Applies to all AI initiatives under the AI OSI Stack v5 governance scope, including third-party models and internal developments.
- Reinforces segregation between custodial governance artifacts in this repository and operational AEIP materials in the Control Tower.

## Responsibilities
- Approve AI risk appetite statements, policies, and control expectations.
- Review quarterly AI risk dashboards, assurance reports, and incident summaries.
- Oversee materiality assessments and disclosure decisions for AI-related events.
- Evaluate third-party AI dependencies, contractual guardrails, and termination criteria.
- Sponsor director and executive education on emerging AI regulations and ethical considerations.

## Information Rights & Cadence
- Receive quarterly AI governance packets including risk register updates, control effectiveness narratives, and incident response outcomes.
- Obtain immediate notification of potential material AI incidents with day 1, day 3, and day 7 updates.
- Access mapping documents linking board oversight to SEC, NACD, and Nasdaq expectations.
- Request ad hoc briefings from the custodian, CRO, or AI Risk Council as needed.

## Delegations to Management
- Management’s AI Risk Council executes policies, maintains operational controls, and reports deviations.
- CRO ensures integration with enterprise risk management and coordinates with disclosure committee.
- Custodian maintains documentation quality, version control, and cross-references across this repository.

## Annual Self-Assessment Questions
1. Did the Board adequately review and approve AI risk appetite and policies?
2. Were committee charters and oversight responsibilities clear and coordinated?
3. Did directors receive timely and decision-useful AI governance information?
4. Were incident responses executed according to the 72-hour protocol?
5. Did third-party oversight identify and mitigate emerging supply chain risks?
6. Were disclosure decisions aligned with SEC expectations and materiality thresholds?
7. Did director education sessions address current regulatory and ethical issues?
8. Were stakeholder concerns appropriately captured and addressed in governance updates?
9. Did the Board challenge management on residual AI risks and remediation plans?
10. Are enhancements to controls, metrics, or reporting required for the upcoming year?
